About the SAT Exam

The SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test) is a standardized test widely used for college admissions in the United States and other countries. The SAT evaluates mathematical, reading, and writing skills and is administered by the College Board.

Purpose: To assess high school students' readiness for college and provide colleges with a common data point for comparison.

SAT Eligibility Criteria

  • No official eligibility criteria set by the College Board.
  • Typically taken by students in high school, preferably in grades 11 and 12.
  • No age limit, but students younger than 13 need parental approval to register.

SAT Exam Structure

The SAT comprises three main sections:

  • Reading: 52 questions - 65 minutes.
  • Writing and Language: 44 questions - 35 minutes.
  • Mathematics: 58 questions - 80 minutes (calculator and non-calculator portions).

Total Duration: 3 hours.

Scoring: SAT scores range from 400 to 1600 points (combining Math and Evidence-Based Reading/Writing).
